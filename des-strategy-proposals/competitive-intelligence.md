## Competitor Profiles

### 1. Dental Intelligence
**Category:** Practice analytics / data intelligence  
**Model:** SaaS subscription (~$400–800/month). Software you manage yourself.  
**What it does:** Monitors practice KPIs — production, collections, new patient count, hygiene retention rate, case acceptance. Benchmarks your numbers against other practices in their network.  
**Integrates with:** Dentrix, Eaglesoft, OpenDental, Carestream  
**Genuine strength:** Best-in-class analytics. If a dentist wants to understand *why* their numbers are off, this is the tool. Deep data, good visualization.  
**Core weakness:** Tells you what's wrong — doesn't fix it. Requires training, buy-in from staff, and ongoing effort to act on the data. Zero marketing execution. Zero patient communication. Zero SEO.  
**Target customer:** Multi-provider practices and DSO groups that have a manager or analyst to act on data.  
**Best used when a prospect says:** "I've been using Dental Intelligence for a year but my numbers haven't improved."

**Proposal language (DentalEdge vs. Dental Intelligence):**  
> "Dental Intelligence is an excellent analytics tool — it shows you exactly where your practice is losing money. But seeing the problem and fixing the problem are two different things. We take those same insights and do the work: we fill the no-show gaps, reactivate the dormant patients, and launch the campaigns that move the numbers — so you don't need to hire an analyst or spend evenings in dashboards."

### 2. Weave
**Category:** Patient communication platform  
**Model:** SaaS ~$400–600/month + phone system. Software you manage yourself.  
**What it does:** Replaces the practice phone system with a VoIP system built for dental. Adds 2-way texting, appointment reminders, review requests, online scheduling, and payments.  
**Integrates with:** Most major PMS systems  
**Genuine strength:** Industry-leading phone + texting experience. Reliable. Practices that switch from a traditional phone system see immediate operational improvement.  
**Core weakness:** Communication tool, not a growth system. No marketing strategy. No SEO. No paid ads. No website. No AI voice agent for after-hours. No local market expertise. Staff still manages the platform daily.  
**Target customer:** Any practice wanting to modernize their phone system and add texting.  
**Best used when a prospect says:** "We've had Weave for two years but we're still not growing."

**Proposal language (DentalEdge vs. Weave):**  
> "Weave handles calls. We handle growth. Those aren't the same thing. Weave is a communication platform — it improves how you talk to patients who already know you. We build the system that gets you new patients in the first place, keeps your existing ones coming back, and manages your reputation so you win the Google search before anyone picks up the phone."

### 3. RevenueWell
**Category:** Dental marketing automation platform  
**Model:** SaaS platform + optional managed services add-ons. Marketing platform starts ~$300–500/month.  
**What it does:** Patient communication, email/text campaigns, reputation management, website design, SEO, online scheduling, digital forms. Dental-specific content library built in.  
**Integrates with:** Dentrix, Eaglesoft, OpenDental, Curve Dental  
**Genuine strength:** Dental-specific, deep PMS integration, good campaign templates, strong reputation management tools. One of the most comprehensive dental-focused SaaS options.  
**Core weakness:** Still primarily a platform your staff manages. Campaign results depend heavily on how much time your team puts in. National company — no local market expertise. Not truly done-for-you.  
**Target customer:** Practices with a dedicated marketing coordinator or office manager willing to work the platform.  
**Best used when a prospect says:** "We tried RevenueWell but our team never had time to actually use it."

**Proposal language (DentalEdge vs. RevenueWell):**  
> "RevenueWell gives you excellent tools — but tools only work if someone uses them. If your front desk doesn't have time to run campaigns, update content, and monitor results, the subscription becomes shelfware. We handle all of that for you. Same capabilities, zero staff time required. And unlike RevenueWell, we know your specific market — we understand what drives patients in Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach, not just generic dental demographics."

**Target customer:** Tech-forward, growth-minded practices with staff capacity to implement and manage a new platform.  
**Best used when a prospect says:** "We just signed up for Adit but we don't know where to start."

**Proposal language (DentalEdge vs. Adit):**  
> "Adit is powerful practice management software — it can do a lot. But software doesn't run itself. You still need someone who knows what to turn on, how to configure it for your specific practice, and how to monitor results. We build and manage the entire system for you — and we go beyond operations into active patient acquisition, SEO, and reputation growth that Adit simply doesn't cover."

## The Four Objections You'll Hear — and How to Respond

**"We already have Weave / RevenueWell / Adit."**  
> "That's good to know. Those are solid tools. The question is: who's managing it for you? If your team is running those platforms on top of their regular work, the system probably isn't being used to its potential. We take that entire burden off your team and run it — plus add the pieces those platforms don't cover."

**"We can't afford a managed service."**  
> "I understand. Let me show you what one empty chair per week costs your practice. [calculate using their chair count]. Most practices recover our entire monthly fee from just 2–3 additional patients per month. The question isn't whether you can afford us — it's whether you can afford to keep losing those patients."

**"We tried a marketing agency before and it didn't work."**  
> "What agency were they? [Listen]. Most marketing agencies build your website or run your ads — then hand you a report and walk away. We don't hand you anything. We run the system. If something isn't working, we fix it. You don't manage us. We manage results."

**"We need to think about it."**  
> "Of course. What specifically do you want to think through? [Listen]. I want to make sure you have everything you need to make the right decision for your practice."

## How This Document Feeds the Proposal System

When Claude generates a personalized proposal, it receives:
- This full competitive intelligence document as context
- The prospect's intake form data (practice name, owner, city, pain points, current tools)
- Data4SEO data (their Google rating, review count, local ranking)

Claude then:
1. Personalizes pain-point framing with their actual numbers
2. Selects the relevant competitor comparison based on what they listed in the intake form
3. Calculates their specific revenue impact (chair count × no-show rate × avg production)
4. Names their actual local competitor with data comparison

**Intake form field that triggers this:** "What tools or services are you currently using or considering?"
