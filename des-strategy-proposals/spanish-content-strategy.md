# Spanish-Language Content Strategy
## DentalEdge Solutions — South Florida Market
**Date:** May 2026  
**Scope:** Spanish content strategy for (1) DentalEdge's own marketing and (2) client implementation playbook

---

## Why This Matters More Than Most Agencies Admit

Most dental marketing agencies offer a translated website as their "bilingual strategy." That's not a strategy — it's a checkbox.

Spanish-speaking patients in South Florida don't just want content in their language. They want to feel that a practice understands their community, communicates like they do, and won't make them feel like a second-tier patient for not speaking English. The practices that get this right don't just attract Spanish-speaking patients — they earn their loyalty and their referrals, which are the most valuable patients in any practice.

DentalEdge's Spanish content strategy is built around this insight: **language is a trust signal, not a translation task.**

## Market Profile: Who We're Writing For

### South Florida Spanish Speaker Breakdown

| Market | Hispanic % | Dominant Heritage | Dialect Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hialeah | ~95% | Cuban-American | Formal register, direct, fast-paced |
| Homestead / South Dade | ~70% | Cuban + Central American | Slightly more formal; Honduran/Guatemalan influence |
| Doral | ~75% | Venezuelan + Colombian | More neutral Latin American Spanish |
| Coral Gables | ~60% | Cuban + mixed | Educated, bilingual, code-switch freely |
| Pembroke Pines / Miramar (Broward) | ~45% | Colombian, Venezuelan, Puerto Rican | Mixed register; more informal than Dade |
| West Palm Beach | ~25% | Puerto Rican, Mexican, mixed | More informal; "tú" acceptable |

### Language Register for South Florida

**Use:** Cuban-adjacent formal Spanish as the default register  
- Address patients as **"usted"** in formal content (website, emails, SMS)
- Use **"tú"** only on social media where informal register is expected
- Avoid overly Mexican or Spanish (Spain) phrasing — it reads as foreign
- Avoid overly clinical language — patients want warmth, not medical textbooks

**Tone model:** How a trusted family friend who happens to be a doctor would talk. Warm, confident, knowledgeable — not corporate, not clinical, not translated-feeling.

## The Four Layers of Spanish Content

### Layer 1: Google Business Profile (Highest Impact — Do First)

The GBP description is the single highest-ROI Spanish content investment. A bilingual GBP description ranks for Spanish-language searches without requiring a website change.

**What to update for each client:**

**Business Description (Spanish + English):**
- Write the first 250 characters in Spanish (this is what appears in Maps preview)
- Follow with English translation
- Include primary Spanish keywords naturally: "dentista en [city]," "limpieza dental," relevant services

**Example template (Hialeah/Cuban market):**
> *En Palmetto Dental Studio, ofrecemos atención dental de calidad para toda la familia — en español y en inglés. Especializados en limpiezas, implantes, blanqueamiento y ortodoncia. Aceptamos la mayoría de los seguros. Llámenos hoy. // Palmetto Dental Studio offers quality dental care for the whole family in Spanish and English. Specializing in cleanings, implants, whitening, and orthodontics. Most insurance accepted.*

**Services section:** Add Spanish versions of every service name  
- Limpieza dental (Teeth Cleaning)  
- Implantes dentales (Dental Implants)  
- Blanqueamiento dental (Teeth Whitening)  
- Ortodoncia / Invisalign (Orthodontics / Invisalign)  
- Carillas dentales (Dental Veneers)  
- Extracción de muelas (Tooth Extraction)  
- Emergencia dental (Dental Emergency)  
- Dentista para niños (Pediatric Dentist)

**Q&A seeding (Spanish):** Add 5 pre-answered questions in Spanish  
- ¿Hablan español en este consultorio? → Sí, atendemos en español e inglés.  
- ¿Aceptan Medicaid / seguro dental? → [specific to each practice]  
- ¿Cuánto cuesta una limpieza dental? → [specific + mention payment plans]  
- ¿Atienden emergencias dentales el mismo día? → [if applicable]  
- ¿Atienden niños? → [if applicable]

**Photos:** Add at least 3 photos with Spanish alt-text and captions in GBP

**Deployment time:** 2–3 hours per client. Do this in Month 1 for all three pilot clients.

One Spanish-language landing page per client, targeting their primary neighborhood keyword. This is not a full website translation — it's a focused, conversion-oriented page for high-intent Spanish searches.

**Priority order:**
1. Smile Plus (Homestead) — "dentista en Homestead"
2. Palmetto Dental Studio (Hialeah) — "dentista en Hialeah"

- H1 must include "dentista en [city]"
- Minimum 600 words of Spanish body content
- hreflang tag: `<link rel="alternate" hreflang="es" href="/dentista-en-[city]" />`

### Layer 3: GHL Follow-Up Sequences in Spanish

The fastest way to lose a Spanish-speaking lead is to follow up in English. Language mismatch in follow-up = 60–70% drop in response rate.

**Required sequences for each client:**

**Sequence A — New Lead (Form Fill)**  
*Trigger: Form submitted, language = Spanish (or name indicates Spanish-speaking)*

- **Day 0, immediate:** SMS in Spanish  
  > "Hola [Nombre], gracias por contactar a [Práctica]. Un miembro de nuestro equipo le llamará pronto. ¿Prefiere que le contactemos en español? Responda SÍ o NO."

- **Day 0 + 2 hours:** If no call booked → second SMS  
  > "¿Todavía le interesa programar su cita en [Práctica]? Reservar es fácil — solo responda a este mensaje o llámenos al [número]. Atendemos en español."

- **Day 1:** Email (Spanish)  
  Subject: `Su consulta con [Práctica] — estamos aquí para ayudarle`  
  Body: Brief warm intro, services overview, easy booking CTA

- **Day 3:** SMS reminder if no booking  
- **Day 7:** Final touch — value email ("5 cosas que debe saber antes de su primera visita al dentista")

**Sequence B — Post-Visit Review Request (Spanish)**  
*Trigger: Visit marked complete in GHL*

- **Same day, evening:** SMS  
  > "Gracias por visitarnos hoy en [Práctica], [Nombre]. Esperamos que su experiencia haya sido excelente. ¿Podría tomarse 1 minuto para dejarnos una reseña en Google? Su opinión ayuda a otras familias a encontrarnos. [link]"

- **Day 3:** If no review → follow-up SMS  
  > "[Nombre], ¿cómo estuvo su visita con nosotros? Si tuvo una buena experiencia, una reseña de Google nos ayudaría muchísimo. Solo toma un minuto. [link] ¡Gracias!"

**Sequence C — Reactivation (Lapsed Patient, Spanish)**  
*Trigger: No visit in 12+ months*

- **SMS:**  
  > "Hola [Nombre], hace un tiempo que no le vemos en [Práctica]. ¿Todo bien? Le recordamos que es importante mantener sus limpiezas dentales al día. ¿Desea programar una cita? Llámenos o responda a este mensaje."

**GHL setup note:** Create a custom field "Preferred Language" (English / Spanish / Both) — collected on the intake form. Route Spanish-preferred contacts automatically into Spanish sequences. Default to Spanish for all South Dade and Hialeah contacts unless specified otherwise.

### Layer 4: Ongoing Content — Blog & Social

This is a lower-priority layer but builds long-term authority. Start after Layers 1–3 are deployed.

**Blog Content Calendar (Monthly — per client or shared on DentalEdge's own blog)**

Month 1: `¿Cuándo fue la última vez que fue al dentista? Lo que pasa si espera demasiado`  
Month 2: `Implantes dentales vs. dentadura postiza: ¿cuál es mejor para usted?`  
Month 3: `¿Cómo escoger un dentista en [City]? 5 preguntas que debe hacer`  
Month 4: `Todo lo que necesita saber sobre el blanqueamiento dental`  
Month 5: `¿Su hijo tiene miedo al dentista? Consejos para padres`  
Month 6: `¿Invisalign o brackets? La guía honesta para adultos`

**Content rules:**
- Write at 8th-grade Spanish reading level — accessible, not dumbed down
- Include one local reference per post (city name, neighborhood, local landmark reference)
- Every post ends with CTA: "¿Tiene preguntas? Llámenos — atendemos en español."
- Minimum 800 words, 1 internal link, 1 image with Spanish alt text

**Social Media (Facebook — primary channel for Spanish-speaking dental patients)**

Facebook dominates over Instagram for this demographic (35+ Cuban-American families in South Florida).

**Content mix (weekly, per client):**
- 2x educational posts in Spanish (dental tips, myth-busting, seasonal reminders)
- 1x social proof post (share a Spanish-language Google review with permission)
- 1x promotional post (seasonal offer, referral incentive)
- Response policy: Always reply to Spanish comments in Spanish, same day

**Post example:**
> ¿Sabía que una limpieza dental cada 6 meses puede prevenir problemas que cuestan miles de dólares? En [Práctica] atendemos en español y aceptamos la mayoría de los seguros. Llame hoy para su cita: [número] 🦷

## Quality Control Rules

1. **Never use Google Translate.** Every piece of Spanish content must be written or reviewed by a native speaker. Machine-translated dental content is immediately detectable and destroys trust faster than no Spanish content at all.

2. **Match the dialect to the market.** Hialeah gets Cuban-register Spanish. Pembroke Pines gets a more neutral Latin American register. Do not use the same copy across markets.

3. **Respond to Spanish reviews in Spanish.** A practice that posts Spanish content but responds to Spanish reviews in English signals inauthenticity. Every Spanish Google review gets a Spanish response within 48 hours.

4. **Track separately.** Spanish content performance should be tracked independently in GHL and Google Analytics — Spanish vs. English new patient conversion rates, review response rates, and keyword rankings. Without separate tracking, the ROI of Spanish content is invisible.

5. **Ask before assuming.** The intake form should always ask preferred language. Do not assume Spanish preference based on name alone — many third-generation Cuban-Americans prefer English. Let the patient tell you.
